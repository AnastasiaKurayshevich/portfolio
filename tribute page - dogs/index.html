Facts about dogs

  • Dogs are a subspecies of the gray wolf, and they are also related to foxes and jackals.
  • Barking is a way for dogs to communicate with other dogs and with humans.
  • Characteristics of loyalty, friendship, protectiveness, and affection have earned dogs an important position in Western society
  • In ancient Egypt dogs were thought to possess godlike characteristics.
  • All dogs have 78 chromosomes, or 39 pairs of chromosomes (humans have 23 pairs), and one member of each pair comes from each parent.
  • The eyesight of a dog is not as keen as its sense of smell, and it is generally thought that dogs have poor colour perception.
